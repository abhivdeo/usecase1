Use Case Brief

Prepared for Seekr — Solution Discovery
Confidential

InSolare Energy Limited

We operate two distinct technology platforms — one for electrolyzer R&D and one for solar plant O&M — and are seeking AI-powered solutions that can enhance intelligence, reduce manual effort, and surface actionable insights from the data these platforms generate. The use cases below describe our current context and where we believe AI can create the most immediate value.

01
R&D Experiment Intelligence for Electrolyzer Development
Electrolyzer Test Platform (ETP) · InSolare Energy Limited
Web App (ETP) 16-parameter cell design Session-based test data CSV / XLSX ingestion Stack monitoring

Our Electrolyzer Test Platform manages the full R&D lifecycle — from registering individual cells (defined by 16 material parameters across membrane, catalyst, bipolar plate, and gasket specifications) through to uploading multi-session test data and viewing live KPIs. Each test session produces performance readings including stack efficiency, specific energy consumption (SEC), H₂ purity, and cell voltage.


Currently, researchers compare cell configurations manually through a tabular "Compare Setups" view and interpret charts session-by-session. With dozens of possible material combinations and multiple test sessions per cell design, identifying which configurations consistently deliver superior performance is slow and insight-dependent on the researcher's own intuition.

Current Challenges
  • Manual cross-session comparison of 16-parameter cells
  • No automated correlation between materials and outcomes
  • AI insights panel exists but lacks deep reasoning
  • Hard to identify best-performing configurations at a glance
AI Opportunity
  • Natural language querying across experiment sessions
  • Correlate material parameters to SEC, efficiency, purity
  • Auto-rank cell designs by KPI performance
  • Highlight anomalous sessions vs. statistical baseline
Example Questions to Answer
  • "Which catalyst achieved lowest SEC across IrO₂ vs IrRuOx tests?"
  • "Rank all cells by H₂ purity deviation from baseline"
  • "What membrane thickness correlates with best efficiency?"
→
Expected outcome: Researchers can query experiment history in natural language, auto-discover high-performing material combinations, and reduce time-to-insight from hours of manual comparison to seconds — accelerating the electrolyzer R&D cycle.
02
Proactive Fault & Performance Intelligence for Solar O&M
App-Based Central Monitoring System (ACMS) · InSolare Energy
Mobile App + Dashboard Multi-site solar fleet Daily generation logs PM checklists Fault reporting

InSolare operates a portfolio of solar power plants and is transitioning from fragmented Excel-based O&M reporting to a centralized ACMS platform. The system captures daily generation data, inverter-wise performance, PR (Performance Ratio), CUF, preventive maintenance checklists, and fault events — all structured and role-governed.


Despite structured data capture, the platform today is largely reactive: faults are logged after they occur, escalation runs through WhatsApp and email, and cross-site pattern analysis is absent. The Phase 3 roadmap explicitly calls for AI — predictive maintenance, anomaly detection, and fleet benchmarking — but there is no AI intelligence layer defined yet. Seekr has an opportunity to define and own this layer.

Current Challenges
  • Reactive fault detection — no alerts until generation drops
  • Manual escalation via WhatsApp / email
  • No cross-site pattern analysis for recurring failures
  • PR deviation and inverter trips go undiagnosed
  • Preventive maintenance compliance tracked manually
AI Opportunity
  • Anomaly detection on PR deviation and generation shortfall
  • Intelligent fault pattern recognition across fleet
  • Predictive signals for inverter failures and PM overdue risk
  • Natural language querying of O&M and generation data
  • Auto-prioritized alert triage for monitoring teams
Example Queries to Enable
  • "Which sites have PR below target for 3+ consecutive days?"
  • "List inverters with repeated trips in the last 30 days"
  • "Show sites with overdue quarterly PM tasks this month"
  • "What's the generation loss attribution by fault type?"
→
Expected outcome: The O&M team shifts from reactive incident logging to proactive intelligence — reducing fault response time, improving plant availability and PR, and giving management a fleet-wide AI layer that continuously monitors performance against targets.
